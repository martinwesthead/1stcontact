## Seed lessons — 2026-07-01 (from the founding design conversation)

### A. "Expensive vs template" tells (rubric seeds)
- **Text over imagery.** Expensive sites lay text over background images with an overlay for legibility. Solid-colour-only heroes read cheap/template. *(Biggest gap found; drives [[REQ-14]].)*
- **Distinctive typography.** Serif display faces / considered pairings (faelan.com: Fraunces + Cormorant Garamond + Playfair) read expensive; `system-ui`/default reads template.
- **Motion.** Purposeful entrance / scroll-reveal / hover is the single biggest "alive vs static-template" signal — on every polished site. *([[REQ-16]].)*
- **Considered, limited colour.** Dark palettes + glow/gradient accents (Composio, PlayerZero); bold gradients (Cartesia); or generous whitespace that lets content breathe (Cohere). Confidence + restraint.
- **Human/organic touches.** Hand-drawn illustration to counter sterile-AI sameness (Anthropic).
- **Alternating band surfaces.** Subtle alternating section backgrounds (gigabytealchemy.ai: cream ↔ darker cream) read as *designed*.
- **Art-directed heroes.** Layered/overlapping imagery with per-image edge treatments (circle crop, torn edge, soft/feathered edge), slight rotation, overlay, hover micro-interaction (faelan.com). Free positioning + treatments = "designed, not templated." *([[REQ-15]].)*

### B. Structural / style-scope lessons
- **Colours are relative to their band.** Text colour is chosen against the section's background ("white text *because* dark image"); global tokens can't hold this — reason per style-scope band. *([[DOC-13]].)*
- **Real sites are vertical stacks of style-scope bands.** Most small-business sites (bakery, gigabytealchemy.ai) are conventional band-stacks → covered by standard modules. "Wow" is a *separate axis* (the ceiling corollary, [[REQ-19]]).
- **Edge treatments are cheap.** Circle = `border-radius`; soft = radial `mask-image`; torn = just a pre-torn PNG asset (mirror the byte — no primitive needed).

### C. Comps (taste reference set)
- **Tier 1 — expensive, template-free (flagship target):** sycamore.so, Composio (dark/motion), Cartesia (bold type/gradient/motion), PlayerZero (dark/glow), Cohere (minimal/breathing), Anthropic (hand-drawn/human), Stability (image-led/confident).
- **Tier 2 — WebGL/3D frontier (bespoke, later):** F1/OFF+BRAND (3D helmet + cinematic scroll, Awwwards Site of the Year 2025), Bruno Simon (drive-a-car Three.js+physics portfolio), Lusion, Immersive Garden, Messenger (WebGL planet). Three.js dominates the top end.

### D. Import / reproduction lessons
- **Static fetch is blind on modern sites.** Wix/Shopify hide colours behind `var()` chains and serve hero images as lazy placeholders → must render (headless browser) and read *computed* styles. *([[DOC-13]].)*
- **Import bar is perceptual** — "indistinguishable to the eye," not pixel-perfect; the eyes loop is the acceptance test. Free-valued *structured* positions are what make indistinguishable reproduction possible. *([[REQ-21]].)*

## Open design questions
- **"A site like X"** — reproduce style/effects/structure (yes); content/brand (no). Prompts + tooling must enforce form ≠ content. *([[DOC-15]] §7.)*
- **Homogenization vs template-free** — encode principles + *diverse* references, never a house style. *([[DOC-16]] §7.)*
