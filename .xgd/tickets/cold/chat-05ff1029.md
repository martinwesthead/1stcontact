# Pricing + Product Model — Conversion-Led GTM (captured reasoning, not a frozen plan)

Working session exploring a price-disruption angle against Wix/Squarespace/WordPress.
This is captured *reasoning*, developed live — an alternative/complementary strand to the
$50 AI-Caretaker plan (DOC-4), not a replacement. Related: DOC-19 (faithful-repro runbook),
REQ-17 (bespoke-module lifecycle), REQ-11 (asset CRUD), REQ-9/REQ-7 (versioning/revisions),
REQ-20 (fidelity testing).

## 1. Core thesis: price is *evidence*, not the strategy
- Marginal hosting cost per static site on Cloudflare (Workers static assets / R2, no egress
  fees) is **cents/month** — $10/mo is trivially feasible *on hosting*.
- But competing *on price* is a trap: $10-vs-$20 doesn't move anyone (switching cost ≫ savings
  for a working site), the floor is $0, and incumbents share your cost structure.
- Reframe: the low price is *proof the AI+eyes architecture removes human labor cost*. The pitch
  is "bring any design, get it editable, for less than Wix" — the **demo is the wedge**, price is
  the side effect. Undercutting Wix is a consequence, not the product.

## 2. The real variable cost is AI, not hosting — and it splits
- **Conversion** (reproduce an existing site) → cheap, and **collapses toward config lookup** as
  the example library grows. Wix/SS/WP sites are a bounded, template-based vocabulary; once
  framework modules *span* that vocabulary, conversion = classify-section + map-to-config.
- **Development** (net-new site) → open-ended, expensive. Keep off the flat tier.
- The moat is **framework coverage of the template vocabulary + a reproduction-transform
  library** — a competitor starting today can't convert faithfully for cents. Milestone to chase:
  "do our modules span the Wix section vocabulary yet?" (each uncovered section type = a one-time
  REQ-17 coverage cost; covered = free forever).
- **Fat head vs long tail:** most SMB sites are near-stock templates → auto-convert. A long tail
  is genuinely bespoke → detect low classifier confidence and *quote it / gate to a higher tier*.

## 3. No aggregate cross-subsidy — each retained unit covered *by construction*
Three post-signup guardrails so unit profit isn't a hope about averages:
1. **Annual prepay** — amortizes onboarding over guaranteed revenue, kills churn-before-recoup
   loss, gives float, filters tire-kickers.
2. **Traffic/usage cap** — bounds steady-state variable cost ("$10 ≠ unlimited"; overage →
   upgrade/throttle).
3. **Auto-convert-only gate** — the classifier-confidence check; expensive long-tail sites never
   land on the flat tier. (The one people forget; without it, 1+2 don't save you.)

Elegant property: **guardrails are a function of the cost curve.** Tighten now (high onboarding
cost); relax as conversion collapses toward cents (drop to monthly, loosen caps). Price-steer
(monthly premium vs annual headline), don't mandate.

## 4. Price calls: $0 / $5 / $10
- **Reject $0** — on our own no-cross-subsidy principle (free = paid users funding free users;
  breaks prepay amortization; attracts zero-intent + abuse). Only exception: a radically-
  constrained **billboard** free tier (subdomain + "Made with" badge + tiny cap, marginal cost
  ~$0, job = advertising, not product).
- **Default $10, not $5** — neither is a budget decision for a business, so price isn't the
  conversion lever (the demo is); take the higher margin + slack on cost variance.
- **Spend the $5 only as a deliberate commoditization weapon** against Wix's entry tier — a
  *different objective* (measured in incumbent pain + funnel volume, not unit margin), and a move
  to make from strength once the expand-to-caretaker path is proven.
- Net: **$10/yr-prepaid, capped, auto-convert-gated**, as a break-even-or-better *land* tier whose
  real product is the expansion to the $50 caretaker.

## 5. CAC = the one legitimately speculative cost (pre-signup free conversion)
- To convert someone you must *show* them their site rebuilt before they pay → speculative spend
  before any guardrail. This is **CAC**, and acquisition is the one place "aggregate covers
  individual" is legitimate (it's inherently probabilistic).
- **Bound it, don't eliminate it:**
  - **Cheap preview before paid fidelity:** pre-signup = fast 80%-fidelity "obviously my site"
    (cents); the expensive DOC-19 values-diff/1c-diff last-5% loop runs *after* signup (behind the
    prepay guardrail).
  - Confidence gate + abuse guard (email-gated, one preview/domain, captcha).
- **Failures are the highest-value output** → CAC doubles as the primary data-acquisition channel.
  Every attempt returns a customer *or* a lesson; three assets from a failure:
  1. transform-improvement signal (classifier misfire vs REQ-17 coverage gap — both compounding),
  2. market intelligence (real demand distribution: builder/industry/quality bar),
  3. a fidelity-benchmark corpus (REQ-20-style, sourced from real demand).
- Caveats: only true if we **instrument + close the loop** (stored bytes nobody trains on = cost);
  payoff is deferred while cash is now (still need a CAC budget pre-flywheel); ToS/copyright/
  privacy of ingesting arbitrary live sites → need a consent/takedown story before public.

## 6. The product spectrum — one product, sliding source material
| Use case | User brings | Engine does |
|---|---|---|
| Migrate | complete design (their site) | reproduce faithfully |
| **Reference-driven** ("sites I like") | exemplars (others' sites) | **extract + blend onto their content** |
| Net-new ("my mousetrap") | intent + content only | generate |

- All three run on the **conversion engine**. Reference sites become a *design brief the AI can
  see* — the thing Wix structurally cannot do (requires understanding design).
- **Net-new collapses toward reference-driven because of the corpus:** the 10k converted sites are
  the raw material to synthesize starting points ("you sell hardware → here are 3 directions").
  Conversion is the R&D that makes development cheap.
- **Reference-driven is the strategic center of gravity** (highest-leverage, most differentiated);
  migration is the sharp cheap *wedge*.

## 7. Editing model — conversation-primary, no visual builder
Principle: **conversation for what's hard to point at; direct-selection for what's hard to
describe.**
1. **Conversational AI core** — can do anything incl. add new modules (REQ-17); this is where the
   expensive open-ended cost lives.
2. **Select-and-edit modal** — view mode → select region → modal with basic-markdown text editor
   or tiny image editor (crop/rotate/brightness only; resist Photoshop creep). Every rendered
   element traces to a structured config node → precise, safe, node-scoped edits (Wix can't).
   **These inline edits must NOT touch the AI** (free margin; the bulk of edit volume).
3. **Asset upload/delete** (REQ-11) — delete with reference-check ("used in 2 places").
4. **Version control** (REQ-9/7) — reframed as the **seatbelt** that makes an all-powerful
   conversational editor safe; semantic diffs ("added testimonials section"), not opaque visual
   ones → AI changes reviewable = trustable.
- **Line = content/surface inline; structure/layout/new-modules conversational.**

## 8. No templates — but own the cold-start job templates were doing
Templates exist because Wix can't generate/understand design; we can → obsolete for us. But they
solve the **blank-page problem** we still have. Better answer: example sites (inspiration, not
forks) + reference-driven start + **corpus-generated personalized starting points** (retrieval +
light assembly to keep cheap). Anti-template is right; don't let it mean "no answer to the blank
page."

## 9. The unifying insight — pricing falls out of "does it touch the AI?"
- **Doesn't touch AI → flat cheap tier ($10):** hosting, inline content edits, image crop, asset
  swap, version restore. Near-zero marginal cost = the "commoditize basic hosting + site" tier.
- **Touches AI → metered/credit tier:** conversational development, reference-driven builds,
  new modules, big redesigns. Open-ended → *not* unlimited on $10.
- The conversion/development split, the cheap-flat/metered split, and the direct-manipulation/
  conversational split are **the same boundary drawn three times** → architecture is coherent.

## 10. SPECULATIVE / flagged-uncomfortable — outbound cold-conversion play
User's note: "not necessarily how I want to do business… this doesn't work, but capture it."
Idea: fingerprint Wix sites → filter to easy-to-convert (confidence gate) → find owners via
on-site contact + LinkedIn → pre-convert + send "here's your site on us, save $100/yr, just add a
card" → AI outreach → AI chat pages that walk them through benefits + conversion. Token cost is
conversational (cheap), not code-gen; experiment on qualified leads.

Assessment:
- **Why it feels off (real signal):** cold outbound on scraped contact info is a legal minefield —
  US CAN-SPAM permits *compliant* cold email, but EU-GDPR/Canada-CASL are consent-based and
  LinkedIn ToS forbids scraping; "we already rebuilt your site" reads creepy to a meaningful
  fraction (bimodal, uncontrollable); AI-outreach-at-scale burns domain reputation; the $100 hook
  is the *weak* card (switching cost ≫ $100).
- **What's excellent + reusable:** (a) the **qualification engine** — target only sites we can
  demo beautifully for cents, know the demo lands before spending → a novel targeting primitive;
  (b) CAC-as-data-pipeline run in reverse; (c) the AI-guided conversion chat page is
  channel-agnostic — build it regardless.
- **Reframe that works:** flip channel from cold-scrape-outbound to **user-initiated inbound**
  ("paste any URL, watch it rebuild" — same magic, consented, viral, no scraping) as primary;
  reserve outbound for a **small, compliant, US-first, demo-led (not discount-led) experiment**
  with opt-out honored and complaints/deliverability measured on a tiny batch before any scaling.
